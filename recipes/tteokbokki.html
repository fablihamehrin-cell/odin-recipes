The Ultimate Cheese Pull: Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki is the king of Korean street food. Itâ€™s made with chewy cylindrical rice cakes simmered in a thick, sweet, and spicy sauce. Adding a thick layer of melted mozzarella on top takes it to a whole new level of comfort.

Tteokbokki Recipe

Ingredients

The Toppings

  • 500g Korean rice cakes (cylindrical type)
  • 150g Fish cakes, cut into bite-sized triangles
  • 1/2 White onion, thinly sliced
  • 1.5 cups Shredded Mozzarella cheese
  • 1 Green onion, sliced diagonally for garnish

The Essentials

  • 3 cups Water (or anchovy/kelp broth for more depth)
  • 1 tsp Vegetable oil
  • Toasted sesame seeds

The Signature Sauce

  • 3 tbsp Gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tbsp Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 2 tbsp Sugar (adjust for sweetness)
  • 1 tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Garlic, finely minced

Steps :

  1. Prepare the Rice Cakes: If you are using refrigerated or frozen rice cakes, soak them in a bowl of room-temperature water for 15 minutes. This ensures they cook evenly and get that signature "QQ" (chewy) texture without cracking. Drain them and pat dry.
  2. Create the Spicy Base: In a wide, shallow pan or a wok, add your 3 cups of water and whisk in the Gochujang, Gochugaru, sugar, soy sauce, and minced garlic. Stir until the paste is fully dissolved and the liquid is a vibrant red.
  3. The First Simmer: Bring the sauce to a boil over medium-high heat. Once bubbling, add the rice cakes and onions. Let them cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally so the rice cakes don't stick to the bottom of the pan.
  4. Add the Texture: Slide the fish cakes into the pan. Reduce the heat to medium. Continue to simmer for another 5 minutes. You will notice the sauce starting to reduce and thicken into a glossy glaze as the starch from the rice cakes releases.
  5. The Cheese Melt: Once the sauce has thickened enough to coat a spoon, turn the heat down to low. Sprinkle the mozzarella cheese generously across the entire surface. Cover the pan with a lid for 2 minutes to trap the steam and melt the cheese until it's bubbly and stretchy.
How to craft the bowl
  1. The Pour: Use a wide spatula to gently slide the tteokbokki into a dark ceramic bowl. Try to keep the cheese layer on top so it doesn't get lost in the sauce.
  2. The Contrast: Sprinkle your green onions and toasted sesame seeds right in the center. The bright green against the white cheese and red sauce creates a perfect visual "pop."
  3. Serve: Serve it while the pan is still hot! The cheese will stay stretchy for about 10 minutes, which is your window for the perfect "cheese pull" photo for your site.
